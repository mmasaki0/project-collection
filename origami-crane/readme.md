## Overview

This is an origami crane modeled in Fusion and 3D printed in 95A TPU (Thermoplastic Polyurethane). My goal for this model was to adapt origami into a decorative print to increase durability and long-term use, as origami decorations can often get crushed or ruined from humidity. 

[[STEP]](</origami-crane/cad/origami crane.step>) [[3MF]](</origami-crane/cad/origami crane.3mf>)

## Tools Used

- **CAD:** AutoDesk Fusion
- **3D Printer:** Bambu A1 Mini
- **Filament:** OVERTURE TPU 95A

## Highlights

- Initial measurements taken from a real folded paper crane
- Form and thickness adjusted for printability
- Added designed supports to prevent wings from flexing during printing

## What I learned

- Limitations for thin and/or tall TPU prints as they will wobble on the print bed (especially on bed slinger printers like the A1 Mini)
- Experimented with a variety of temperature and retraction settings
- Importance of drying hygroscopic filaments before printing
